🔒 Consent Management Platforms in Android Applications

Master's Research Internship · 2022

This project investigated how mobile applications implement consent management mechanisms to comply with modern privacy regulations such as the GDPR. While consent management has been extensively studied on the web, mobile applications remain less explored despite their widespread collection and sharing of personal data.

Through large-scale analysis and experimentation, I studied how Android applications integrate Consent Management Platforms (CMPs), identified common CMP providers, and explored methods for detecting their presence within applications. The study also examined potential privacy risks associated with these platforms and highlighted future research directions for privacy transparency in mobile ecosystems.

🛡 Securing Event Streaming Platforms for IoT using Apache Kafka

Master's Research Project · 2021

This project explored security challenges in outsourced event-streaming infrastructures for IoT applications. While Apache Kafka provides a scalable and reliable platform for processing large volumes of data, sensitive information remains exposed to third-party infrastructure providers.

To address this issue, we designed and implemented a privacy-preserving matching scheme integrated with Apache ZooKeeper. The proposed approach enables producers and consumers to communicate through encrypted topic identifiers, allowing authorized entities to discover and subscribe to data streams without revealing the original topic names to the Kafka server.

The project included the implementation of the matching algorithm as a reusable Java library, the development of Kafka producer and consumer applications, and the simulation of secure communication across multiple Kafka servers. This work demonstrates how confidentiality can be enhanced in distributed event-streaming systems while preserving scalability.

📱 Trusted Food Supply Chain System using QR Code Verification

Bachelor's Capstone Project (Final Year Project) · 2017

This capstone project focuses on building a complete food traceability system that connects producers, transporters, sellers, and consumers through QR code-based verification.

The system consists of a management web platform operated by a competent authority, where producers, transporters, and sellers must register their product-related information. All submitted data is verified and approved by an administrator before being validated in the system.

Once approved, a unique QR code is automatically generated and sent to the registrant via email. This QR code is attached to the physical product and serves as a secure identifier linking the product to its entire supply chain history.

Consumers can scan the QR code using an Android application to access full product information, including details about the producer, transporter, seller, and origin of the product, ensuring transparency and trust in the food supply chain.
